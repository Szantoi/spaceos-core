# Campaign Management Knowledge Article

This article provides guidance for planning, launching, and analyzing marketing campaigns across channels (email, social, paid, and in-product).

## Key Concepts

### Campaign Objective

Every campaign needs a clear objective with measurable key results. Common examples:

- **Lead generation:** Increase MQLs by 20% month-over-month.
- **Activation:** Increase feature adoption by 15% within 30 days.
- **Retention:** Reduce churn by 10% in the next quarter.

Define **success metrics** up front (e.g., conversion rate, average order value, CAC) and align them to stakeholders.

### Audience Segmentation

Segment audiences based on attributes like:

- Behavior (e.g., recent active users, trial users, power users)
- Demographics (e.g., industry, company size, role)
- Intent signals (e.g., feature usage, page views, intent keywords)

Use a **single source of truth** for segment definitions to ensure consistent targeting across channels.

### Campaign Assets & Briefs

Create a campaign brief to document:

- Campaign goal and success criteria
- Target audience definitions
- Messaging pillars and content direction
- Channel mix and delivery plan (email, ads, in-product)
- Measurement plan (events, reports, dashboards)

Store campaign artifacts in the knowledge base so they can be reused and audited.

### Measurement & Iteration

Track performance daily during launch and iteratively optimize:

- Monitor leading indicators (click-through rate, open rate, ad frequency)
- Validate hypothesis with A/B tests (headline, CTA, timing)
- Document learnings and next actions in a shared handoff artifact

### Compliance & Privacy

Ensure campaigns follow privacy policies and data handling requirements:

- Do not use PII without explicit consent
- Respect user opt-out signals (e.g., do-not-contact lists)
- Partner with Legal/Privacy for new data sources or tracking changes
